Bull

"Bulls are male cattle known for their strength and typically larger size compared to cows. They play a significant role in agriculture, primarily for breeding purposes and sometimes in activities like bullfighting or rodeos."

Unique traits and behaviors

"Bulls are known for their muscular build and aggressive behavior, especially during mating seasons when they might display dominance through actions like charging or bellowing. They often establish a hierarchy within their herd."

Food

Grass, hay, grains, and other vegetation

Habitat

Open Grasslands

Extensive agricultural landscapes such as the United States, Brazil, India, and China.

Fiber Production

Some cattle breeds, such as Angus and Hereford, are primarily raised for meat production rather than fiber. However, specific breeds like Highland cattle are valued for their long, thick hair used for fiber production.

Lifespan

The lifespan of a bull varies depending on factors like breed, health care, and living conditions. On average, they can live for 15-20 years.

Environmental Adaptability:

Bulls exhibit adaptability to various climates and environments, though some breeds might be better suited to certain conditions. They can withstand different temperatures but might require specific care in extreme weather.

Increasing Popularity

Bulls continue to be crucial in agriculture for breeding purposes, especially in meat and dairy production. However, the popularity might vary based on cultural practices and preferences, with some regions favoring bull-related sports or events. Overall, they maintain their significance in various agricultural and cultural contexts.
