Stag

"Stags are male deer, recognized for their majestic antlers and significant size compared to female deer, known as does."

Unique traits and behaviors

"Stags showcase impressive antlers used primarily for display during mating seasons. They're known for their agility and can leap gracefully over obstacles while displaying dominance through posturing and vocalizations."

Food

Grass, leaves, twigs, and shrubs.

Habitat

Forests, woodlands, and grasslands.

Europe, Asia, North America, and other continents.

Fiber Production

While stags don't produce fiber directly sought after by humans, their hides can be used in leather production.

Lifespan

Stags typically live around 10-15 years in the wild, with variations depending on factors like species, habitat, and health conditions.

Environmental Adaptability:

Stags are adaptable to diverse environments, from mountainous regions to temperate forests, showcasing resilience in various climates and terrains.

Increasing Popularity

Stags and deer, in general, have gained attention in conservation efforts and eco-tourism, contributing to their increased popularity in wildlife preservation and as subjects of admiration among nature enthusiasts. However, their popularity as hunting game remains a point of contention in different regions.
