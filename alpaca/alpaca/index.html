ALPACA

"Alpacas, gentle South American camelids, are prized for their soft fleece in various colors. Standing around three feet tall and weighing 100-175 pounds, they're known for their calm nature and serve a cultural role in the Andes."

Unique traits and behaviors

"Alpacas boast a few unique traits and behaviors. They're known for their highly social nature, often forming close-knit herds. These animals are efficient grazers, utilizing their padded feet to tread lightly on terrain and eat without damaging grass roots. Additionally, alpacas have a curious habit of humming, using various vocalizations for communication among themselves."
